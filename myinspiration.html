About

Ratan Tata is an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of Tata Sons. He is known for his humility, vision, and contribution to Indian industry and society.

Biography

Born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India, Ratan Tata studied architecture at Cornell University and completed an advanced management course at Harvard Business School. He joined the Tata Group in 1962 and became its chairman in 1991. Under his leadership, the Tata Group transformed into a global brand by launching the Tata Nano and acquiring iconic companies such as Jaguar-Land Rover (JLR), Tetley, and Corus Steel. Ratan Tata is widely respected for his humility, integrity, and vision. Although he retired in 2012, he continues to serve as Chairman Emeritus and remains actively involved in philanthropy.

Key Achievements

  • Launched the Tata Nano the world's cheapest car
  • Oversaw Tata's acquisition of Jaguar Land Rover
  • Awarded Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan
  • Major contributor to education, healthcare & rural development
